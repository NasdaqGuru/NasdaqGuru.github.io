A. Core Performance Areas
Dollars Raised
Total gifts closed (booked) by assigned portfolio. Primary measure of productivity. Weighted by gift type: outright > pledge > planned.
Target: $3.5M–$8M annually (varies by portfolio tier)
Gifts Closed
Number of closed gifts at or above ask amount. Tracks conversion efficiency from proposal to close across all gift ranges.
Target: 18–30 closed gifts/year depending on portfolio
Pipeline Value (12-month)
Total expected value of cultivation prospects rated probable or imminent within the next 12 months. Validated quarterly against CRM activity.
Target: 3× annual goal in active pipeline
Ask-to-Close Ratio
% of formal proposals resulting in a gift. Tracks proposal quality and cultivation depth. Distinguishes between full-ask, partial, and declined outcomes.
Target: ≥60% ask-to-close rate
B. Activity & Engagement Metrics
Meaningful Visits
Substantive in-person or virtual meetings advancing cultivation: qualification, discovery, cultivation, solicitation, or stewardship purpose. Not social calls.
Target: 120–180 meaningful visits/year
Proposals Submitted
Formal written asks delivered to rated prospects. Includes gift range, purpose, impact, and multi-year structure. Reviewed by CDO before submission.
Target: 24–40 proposals/year
New Prospects Identified
Net new donors added to assigned portfolio through discovery visits, alumni event attendance, referrals, or predictive modeling output.
Target: 40–60 new qualified prospects/year
Upgrades Secured
Donors moved to a higher giving level: annual → mid-level, mid-level → major, major → principal. Tracks effectiveness of cultivation strategy.
Target: 15–25 upgrades/year
C. Relationship & Strategic Metrics
Portfolio Engagement Score
Composite score (1–100) measuring depth of relationship: visit frequency, communication responsiveness, event attendance, and giving history. Updated monthly via CRM.
Target: Avg portfolio score ≥65/100
Donor Intent Clarity
% of top-rated prospects with documented philanthropic interest, giving capacity, and affinity scores on file. Ensures proposals are aligned with donor values.
Target: 90% of rated prospects have documented intent
Multi-Year Commitment Rate
% of closed gifts with multi-year pledge or recurring commitment attached. Indicates depth of relationship and revenue predictability.
Target: ≥40% of closed gifts are multi-year
Legacy Identification Rate
% of portfolio rated $1M+ capacity who have been introduced to planned giving. Tracks pipeline for SEAS Legacy Giving program from frontline officers.
Target: 25% of $1M+ prospects discussed planned gifts
D. Advanced Metrics — Relationship Velocity & Predictive Indicators
Cultivation Velocity (days)
Average number of days from first meaningful contact to gift closure by gift range. Tracked by officer and compared to team median. Shorter = stronger cultivation depth.
Best practice: <18 months for $100K–$499K; <36 months for $1M+
RFM-Based Capacity Gap
Difference between actual giving and estimated giving capacity (from wealth screening). Identifies where officers are under-soliciting relative to donor potential.
Flag: donors giving <10% of estimated capacity for 2+ years
Reactivation Rate
% of lapsed donors (18+ months no gift) successfully reengaged with a new gift. Measures effectiveness of win-back strategies and relationship longevity.
Target: ≥25% reactivation within 24 months of lapse
Matching Gift Capture Rate
% of gifts where employee matching gift was identified and successfully claimed. Unclaimed matching gifts represent unrealized revenue — tracked by officer and by employer.
Target: ≥85% matching gift capture for eligible donors
Best Practices for Measurement: KPIs should be reviewed weekly at team huddles (activity metrics), monthly in 1:1s (pipeline and dollars), and quarterly in formal performance reviews (all dimensions). No single metric tells the full story — a high visit count with low conversion rate signals cultivation quality issues, while high dollars with low visits may indicate heavy reliance on a few relationships.

Case Study A: Major Gift Upgrade via Multi-Year Cultivation — Mid-Career Fellow to Endowed Chair
Transformational Gift

A Mid-Career Master in Public Policy alumnus (MPP '04) had been a consistent Annual Fund donor at the $2,500–$5,000 level for 12 consecutive years. Standard RFM scoring flagged this donor as high-frequency, high-recency — but wealth screening estimated capacity at $2M–$5M, suggesting a significant solicitation gap.

The assigned gift officer initiated a discovery visit to understand the donor's philanthropic priorities. Over 18 months, three cultivation visits and two dean's roundtable invitations deepened the relationship. A formal proposal for a $2M endowed Faculty Chair in Public Leadership was submitted, aligning with the donor's expressed interest in developing future public servants.

The gift closed at $2.2M over 5 years, establishing a named chair. The matching gift program added an additional $880K. Total impact: $3.08M. The donor joined the SEAS Leadership Circle and has since referred two colleagues who are now in active cultivation at the $250K+ level.

A Loyalty Society member with an 11-year giving streak (annual gifts of $1,000–$1,500) missed their FY2023 gift — the first gap in over a decade. Standard automated LYBUNT (Last Year But Unfortunately Not This Year) outreach was sent with no response.

The annual giving team flagged this donor to the assigned gift officer due to the streak length and loyalty pattern. A personalized phone call from the officer — acknowledging the streak and asking if there was anything they could do better — revealed the donor had experienced a family health event and felt disconnected from SEAS priorities.

The team arranged a personal note from a faculty member whose research the donor had previously funded. The donor reactivated in FY2024 at $2,500 — an upgrade — and their streak was preserved with a notation. They are now in the Dean’s Innovation Circle discovery pipeline following a campus visit.

Case Study C: Matching Gift Program — Unclaimed Revenue Recovery
Matching Gifts

A data audit of FY2022 and FY2023 gifts revealed that $1.84M in potential matching gifts had gone unclaimed — donors who worked for matching-eligible employers had not submitted match requests. This represented 68 distinct employers across 214 individual gifts.

The development analytics team built a targeted outreach campaign using employer data from the CRM. Personalized emails walked donors through the matching gift submission process, including direct links to their employer's matching portal. A follow-up stewardship call was made for gifts over $5,000.

Within 90 days, $1.24M in matching gifts were recovered — a 67.4% capture rate on the identified pool. The team also identified 42 donors whose employers had changed matching policies, enabling proactive outreach for FY2024 gifts. Annual matching gift capture rate increased from 64% to 82% following the program implementation.

Glossary of Key Terms
TermDefinition
Gift FundA restricted or endowed account established to hold and invest donor contributions for a specific purpose (e.g., named scholarship, research program, endowed chair).
Endowment DistributionThe annual spend from invested endowed funds, typically calculated as a % of the trailing 12-quarter average market value (commonly 4.5–5.5%).
RFM AnalysisRecency-Frequency-Monetary scoring model that ranks donors by how recently they gave, how often they give, and how much they give — used for targeting and upgrade identification.
LYBUNTLast Year But Unfortunately Not This Year — donors who gave in the prior fiscal year but have not yet given in the current year. Key reactivation target group.
SYBUNTSome Year But Unfortunately Not This Year — donors who gave at some point historically but not in the recent cycle. Broader lapsed cohort.
Pledge ReceivableA multi-year commitment by a donor to make a gift in the future, recorded as a receivable on the institution's balance sheet per SEAS accounting standards.
Planned Gift / BequestA deferred charitable gift arranged during a donor's lifetime but realized at death (e.g., bequest in a will, charitable remainder trust, life insurance designation).
Major GiftDefined at SEAS as a gift or pledge of $100,000 or more. Typically managed by assigned major gift officers through personalized cultivation and solicitation.
CASE/VSECouncil for Advancement and Support of Education / Voluntary Support of Education — the industry standard for counting, reporting, and benchmarking philanthropic gifts in higher education.
Ask-to-Close RatioThe percentage of formal solicitations (proposals) that result in a closed gift at any level. Industry best practice: ≥60%.
Cultivation VelocityThe average number of months from first meaningful contact with a gift officer to gift closure. Shorter cycles indicate stronger relationship depth and proposal readiness.
SEAS Leadership CircleSEAS named giving society for donors who have made cumulative lifetime contributions of $100,000 or more to Harvard John A. Paulson School of Engineering and Applied Sciences.
Dean’s Innovation CircleSEAS leadership giving society recognizing donors who contribute $10,000 or more annually. Members receive enhanced stewardship and senior leadership access.
Loyalty SocietySEAS recognition program for donors who have given for 5 or more consecutive fiscal years, regardless of gift size. Celebrates consistency and institutional commitment.
