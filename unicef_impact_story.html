47 countries achieved MNT elimination by late 2019 — protecting millions of mothers and newborns from a wholly preventable disease.

Chronicle of Impact

A decade-and-a-half of milestones

From pilot fund to global force — the journey that brought us here.

2010 – 2011

The Impact Fund for Children is born

The Bridge Fund — later renamed the Impact Fund for Children — was launched to solve a deceptively simple problem: pledges are made, but children can't wait for cash to arrive. By bridging that gap, UNICEF could purchase vaccines and food up to four months sooner.

$741M+ accelerated to date
2012

Revenue crosses $500 million

A watershed year for institutional confidence — UNICEF USA surpasses $500M in total revenue, reflecting growing donor trust and expanded programmatic ambition. Program efficiency ratio holds at 84%.

2019

47 countries achieve MNT elimination

The Eliminate Project, launched in 2010 with Kiwanis International, reaches its landmark goal. Maternal and neonatal tetanus — a scourge of the developing world — is eliminated in 47 countries, protecting entire generations of mothers and newborns.

2023 – 2024

Child Nutrition Fund launched; global crises response

The CNF is established with a $2B by 2030 ambition. Simultaneously, UNICEF responds to 448 emergencies across 104 countries — delivering 1.5 billion polio doses and screening 109 million children for malnutrition.

Revenue: $852M (2024)
March 2025

The Bezos Challenge — a $1 billion moment

Jacklyn and Miguel Bezos commit up to $500 million to the Child Nutrition Fund as a dollar-for-dollar match — UNICEF USA's largest private donation in history. The gift targets 320 million children and women annually by 2030.

Largest private gift in UNICEF USA history
2025

$835M revenue — highest flexible funding ever

Fiscal 2025 closes with $835M in revenue and — critically — the highest-ever proportion of flexible funding. Flexible dollars are the lifeblood of rapid emergency response, enabling UNICEF to pre-position supplies and deploy within hours.

Future Pillars

Five pillars powering the next decade

Every gift you make activates one or more of these five foundational programs — each a promise to a different child, in a different crisis, on a different continent.

Pillar 1

Health & Immunization

24.7M

Children received measles vaccines in 2024. Child mortality has fallen over 50% since 2000.

Pillar 2

Nutrition

441M

Children, pregnant women, and breastfeeding mothers reached with malnutrition prevention in 2024.

Pillar 3

WASH

33M

People gained access to safe water in 2024. 18M gained basic sanitation. 41M reached in humanitarian WASH emergencies.

Pillar 4

Education

26M

Out-of-school children and adolescents gained access to education — 9 million of them in active humanitarian settings where conflict had closed schools. Global innovation portfolios reached 38 million people directly across 118 countries by end of 2024.

Pillar 5

Child Protection

50%↓

Reduction in adolescent girls acquiring HIV since 2010 — driven by UNICEF-supported prevention programs. Child protection spans legal reform, safe spaces, psychosocial support, and anti-trafficking initiatives across over 190 countries.
