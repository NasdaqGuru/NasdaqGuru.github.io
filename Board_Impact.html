Endowment as a future trust

Harvard describes its endowment as a long-term source of funding made up of philanthropic gifts, many restricted to specific teaching, research, or program purposes. The FY2025 endowment value was reported at $56.9 billion.

Access powered by philanthropy

Endowment support helped Harvard grant $784 million in financial aid and scholarships in FY2025, connecting donor generosity directly to student access and affordability. Approximately 55% of undergraduates receive need-based scholarships.

Past: prove that philanthropy compounds.

For a Board or Corporation audience, the past is not a history slide; it is proof that gifts become infrastructure. The narrative should connect donor intent to long-run capacity: scholarship funds, chairs, research platforms, campus renewal, and school-level excellence.

  • Board question: What forms of giving have produced durable institutional advantage?
  • Evidence package: campaign milestones, endowed funds, named professorships, alumni reach, and time-series impact stories.
  • Decision value: show where long-term donor purpose has translated into sustainable mission capacity.
Past-to-present impact bridge

Current: make stewardship operational.

The current-state view should help leadership see whether philanthropic capital is restricted, deployed, under-narrated, or exposed to stewardship risk. It should be clear enough for board review and precise enough for gift administration, finance, and school teams.

  • Control points: donor intent, fund purpose, spend policy, balance movement, program owner, and impact evidence.
  • Operating need: reduce manual report assembly while increasing confidence in definitions and source lineage.
  • Board output: a one-page "what is strong / what needs intervention / what scales next" view.
FY2025 stewardship balance

Future: move from after-action reporting to foresight.

The future view emphasizes readiness: where scholarship demand, research opportunity, donor restrictions, faculty needs, and external risk signals require earlier action. It is where VERITAS turns a static impact report into an intelligence system.

  • Future lenses: access pressure, research acceleration, faculty competitiveness, donor confidence, and flexible capital.
  • Executive use: identify bottlenecks before they become donor, program, or governance risks.
  • Life-balance benefit: reduce emergency reporting cycles by making standard evidence blocks reusable and pre-verified.
Future readiness index · illustrative

Projection: show the next best investment path.

For Corporation-level review, projection should not overstate certainty. It should show scenario logic: base, stretch, and risk-adjusted pathways, with assumptions labeled and tied to program owners.

  • Base case: stewardship narrative and compliance improve with reusable reporting blocks.
  • Stretch case: VERITAS routing accelerates response time and next-best-action adoption.
  • Risk case: unresolved fund restrictions, manual reconciliation, or fragmented data slow donor response.

$695M
Gifts & pledge payments in H1 FY2026 — up from $440M in the same period last year, a +58% surge. FAS alone raised $222M, on track for a record year. SEAS tripled its 4-year average. This is philanthropy absorbing federal risk in real time.
