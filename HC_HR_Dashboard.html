Executive Summary

FY2026 — Harvard College HR Highlights

Engagement declined 3 points to 71%, with Residential Life (62%) and Student Affairs (65%) showing the steepest drops. Root causes include reorganization-related uncertainty and manager effectiveness gaps identified in the Q1 pulse. Targeted interventions are recommended for both units.
14 active ER cases — 3 classified as high-priority (2 active investigations, 1 performance improvement plan). ER caseload is up 28% vs. FY2025 same period, concentrated in Athletics (4 cases) and Residential Life (3 cases).
Performance scores improved to 3.68 avg (↑0.08 vs. FY2025). 87 employees (18.8%) rated "Exceeds Expectations" or higher — a positive signal following the FY2025 manager effectiveness coaching program.
Time-to-fill increased to 6.8 weeks (↑0.9 weeks). Principal driver is increased competition for program administration talent. A Talent Acquisition partnership review with FAS TA is recommended for Q3.
