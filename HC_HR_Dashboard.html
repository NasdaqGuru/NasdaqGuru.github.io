Executive Summary

FY2026 — Harvard College HR Highlights

Engagement declined 3 points to 71%, with Residential Life (62%) and Student Affairs (65%) showing the steepest drops. Root causes include reorganization-related uncertainty and manager effectiveness gaps identified in the Q1 pulse. Targeted interventions are recommended for both units.
14 active ER cases — 3 classified as high-priority (2 active investigations, 1 performance improvement plan). ER caseload is up 28% vs. FY2025 same period, concentrated in Athletics (4 cases) and Residential Life (3 cases).
Performance scores improved to 3.68 avg (↑0.08 vs. FY2025). 87 employees (18.8%) rated "Exceeds Expectations" or higher — a positive signal following the FY2025 manager effectiveness coaching program.
Time-to-fill increased to 6.8 weeks (↑0.9 weeks). Principal driver is increased competition for program administration talent. A Talent Acquisition partnership review with FAS TA is recommended for Q3.

🧮 Methodology & Data Assumptions

Transparent calculation notes for the portfolio dashboard: how synthetic numbers, engagement points, risk scoring, averages, forecast payroll, market rates, and long-range scenarios were created and how they should be validated before real use.

Portfolio mode: no confidential Harvard employee records are used. Values are illustrative and designed to show consulting judgment, not official institutional reporting.
Methodology principle: summary KPIs should trace back to raw rows. The dashboard now separates raw registers from calculated views: raw position payroll, raw ER cases, raw requisitions, raw compensation flags, raw leave records, raw policy risks, raw action items, and raw market-rate references.

📊 Engagement score

Engagement is modeled as a 0–100 index using anonymized pulse dimensions. Unit scores are weighted averages of manager support, workload balance, belonging, clarity, and growth opportunity.

Unit engagement = Σ(dimension score × dimension weight) / Σ(weights)
Overall engagement = Σ(unit engagement × filled FTE) / Σ(filled FTE)
Point change = current score − prior-year score

👥 Headcount, vacancies & fill rate

Authorized FTE, filled positions, and vacancies are modeled at the position level. Vacancy rate and fill rate should be calculated from the authorized position register, not from budget totals alone.

Vacancies = Authorized FTE − Filled FTE
Fill rate = Filled FTE / Authorized FTE
Vacancy rate = Vacant FTE / Authorized FTE

🤝 ER and policy risk scoring

Risk levels are based on a point model that weighs severity, days open, partner involvement, employee impact, repeat pattern, and documentation quality.

Risk score = severity points + aging points + partner/escalation points + repeat-pattern points + documentation-gap points
High ≥ 75 · Medium 45–74 · Low / monitor < 45

💵 Budget vs. forecast payroll

Forecast payroll is derived from raw position rows. Filled roles use current salary plus projected raise; vacant roles use the budgeted salary adjusted by expected start date and temp/overtime offset assumptions.

Filled forecast = current salary × (1 + projected raise %)
Vacant forecast = budget salary × active-months factor + temp/overtime offset
Variance = budget salary − forecast salary

⚖️ Compensation and market rates

Market-rate rows are contextual planning inputs. Official salary setting should anchor to Harvard grade/range, internal equity, comparator roles, current postings, and Compensation review.

Market midpoint = (public low + public high) / 2 when available
Internal equity flag = same role / grade spread > 15% or below band minimum
Market context ≠ salary recommendation

⏳ 5 / 10 / 15 / 20-year scenarios

Long-range scenarios scale from the current authorized FTE baseline using illustrative assumptions for service demand, retirement exposure, future-skills shift, and salary-budget growth.

Projected FTE = baseline FTE + demand-growth assumption
Retirement exposure = age/tenure eligibility estimate × horizon factor
Succession coverage = roles with named backup / critical roles
Budget scenario = salary baseline × inflation / merit / role-growth factors

Scoring rubric used in popups

Risk point guide

  • Severity: 0–30 points based on employee, policy, operational, or reputational impact.
  • Aging: 0–20 points based on days open or urgency window.
  • Pattern: 0–15 points for repeat signals in one unit or job family.
  • Exposure: 0–20 points for finance, staffing, service delivery, or climate impact.
  • Control gap: 0–15 points for missing documentation, owner, or next action.

Interpretation thresholds

  • High: ≥75 points; needs dated action, owner, and escalation path.
  • Medium: 45–74 points; needs monitoring plus manager/partner action.
  • Monitor: below 45 points; keep in trend view or next scheduled review.
  • Engagement: below 65 = intervention zone; 65–74 = watch; 75+ = stable / strength.
